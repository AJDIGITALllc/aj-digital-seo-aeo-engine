# Sales System

**Framework for AJ Digital's sales process — from lead qualification to closed deal to successful onboarding.**

---

## Purpose

Sales is the engine that funds everything else. This skill defines a repeatable, metrics-driven sales process that converts qualified leads into premium clients. It is not about tactics — it is about building a system that works reliably at any volume.

## Principles

### 1. Qualify Hard, Sell Easy

The faster you disqualify poor-fit leads, the more time you spend on deals that close and clients who succeed. Qualification is not rude — it's respectful of everyone's time.

### 2. Sell the Outcome, Not the Deliverable

Clients don't buy a website. They buy more leads, higher revenue, a professional image, or market credibility. Every conversation focuses on the business outcome.

### 3. Price on Value, Not Time

Hourly pricing punishes efficiency and rewards slowness. Value-based pricing aligns incentives: the more valuable the project is to the client, the more it's worth.

### 4. Proposals Are Documents of Alignment

A proposal should confirm what was already discussed and agreed upon — not introduce new information. If a proposal surprises the client, the discovery process failed.

### 5. The Sale Doesn't End at the Signature

Onboarding is the first delivery experience. A smooth onboarding confirms the client made the right decision. A chaotic onboarding creates buyer's remorse.

## Sales Process Framework

### Stage 1: Lead Intake

**Trigger:** Inbound inquiry (form submission, email, referral)

**Actions:**
1. Acknowledge within 4 business hours — speed matters
2. Review the inquiry: what do they need, what's their budget signals, what's their timeline
3. Send a brief response with 2-3 discovery questions and a booking link

**Qualification signals (positive):**
- Has a clear business goal, not just "we need a new website"
- Budget range aligns with our services (not looking for $500 websites)
- Timeline is realistic (not "we need it by next week")
- Decision-maker is involved (not a junior researcher)

**Disqualification signals:**
- Price-shopping (sent the same brief to 10 agencies)
- No clear business goal or success metric
- Budget far below our minimum
- Unrealistic timeline with no flexibility

### Stage 2: Discovery Call

**Duration:** 30–45 minutes

**Structure:**

| Block (min) | Focus                                    |
|-------------|------------------------------------------|
| 0–5         | Set agenda, build rapport                |
| 5–15        | Understand their business and goals      |
| 15–25       | Identify pain points and current state   |
| 25–35       | Discuss what success looks like (metrics)|
| 35–40       | Qualify: budget, timeline, decision process |
| 40–45       | Next steps: what we'll prepare and when  |

**Key questions:**

1. "What's the primary business outcome you're hoping this project achieves?"
2. "What's not working about your current website/system?"
3. "How will you measure whether this project was successful?"
4. "What's the timeline, and what's driving that?"
5. "Who else is involved in making this decision?"
6. "Have you set aside a budget range for this project?"

**After the call:** Send a summary email confirming:
- What they told us about their goals and challenges
- What we'll prepare (proposal, strategy brief, estimate)
- When they'll receive it
- The next step after they receive it

### Stage 3: Proposal

**Timeline:** Deliver within 3-5 business days of discovery call.

**Proposal structure:**

```
1. Executive Summary
   - What they need (in their words)
   - What we'll deliver
   - The business outcome they can expect

2. Understanding
   - Their business, audience, and current challenges
   - Shows we listened and understood

3. Approach
   - Our process for this project (reference workflow.md)
   - Why this approach produces results

4. Scope of Work
   - Specific deliverables, organized by phase
   - What's included and what's not included
   - Milestones and timeline

5. Investment
   - Total project cost (value-based, not hourly)
   - Payment schedule (e.g., 50% upfront, 25% at midpoint, 25% at launch)
   - What's included in ongoing support (if applicable)

6. Why AJ Digital
   - Relevant case studies or results
   - Team qualifications
   - What makes us different for THIS project

7. Next Steps
   - How to proceed (sign agreement, pay deposit)
   - What happens immediately after signing
```

**Proposal rules:**
- Max 6 pages (excluding case study examples)
- Written in their language, not ours
- Clear, specific deliverables — no vague "strategic consulting"
- One clear price — no confusing option matrices
- PDF format, professionally branded

### Stage 4: Follow-Up and Close

**Follow-up cadence:**

| Day After Sent | Action                                         |
|----------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Day 0          | Send proposal with brief personal message      |
| Day 2          | Email: "Wanted to make sure this landed — happy to walk through it" |
| Day 5          | Call or email: "Any questions I can answer?"    |
| Day 10         | Final check: "Want to confirm this is still a priority" |
| Day 14+        | Move to nurture or close as lost               |

**Closing conversation:**
- Address any remaining questions or concerns
- If there are objections, handle them (see below)
- If there are changes needed, revise and resend promptly
- Once agreed, send contract and first invoice within 24 hours

### Stage 5: Onboarding

**Immediately after signing:**

1. **Welcome email** — confirmation, excitement, what happens next
2. **Asset collection** — brand guidelines, access credentials, content, imagery
3. **Project setup** — project management board, shared folder, communication channel
4. **Kickoff call** — align the full team, confirm timeline, review scope
5. **First deliverable** — provide early value within 5-7 business days

## Value-Based Positioning

### Pricing Framework

| Project Type            | Value Indicator                    | Price Range           |
|------------------------|------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| Marketing Website      | Lead generation                     | $10K–$30K            |
| Conversion-Optimized Site | Direct revenue impact            | $20K–$50K            |
| Custom Web Application | Operational efficiency              | $30K–$75K            |
| Full Digital Platform  | Business transformation             | $50K–$100K+          |

### How to Communicate Price

**Never apologize for pricing.** Instead:

1. **Anchor to value:** "Based on your goal of increasing leads by 40%, the investment for this project is $25,000."
2. **Contextualize:** "That's roughly the cost of one new sales hire for one month — but this asset works 24/7 for years."
3. **Break it down:** "$25,000 across a 3-year lifespan is $694 per month — less than most SaaS tools."

## Objection Handling

| Objection                    | Response Approach                                       |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| "It's too expensive"         | Reframe to value: what's the cost of NOT doing this?   |
| "We can get it cheaper"      | Different investments produce different results. Ask what they expect from the cheaper option. |
| "We're not ready yet"        | "When will you be ready? Let's set a timeline."        |
| "We need to think about it"  | "Of course. What specific questions do you need answered?" |
| "Can you do it faster?"      | "Speed and quality are tradeoffs. Here's what's realistic." |
| "We're comparing options"    | "Great — what criteria are you evaluating on?"          |

**Core rule:** Never chase. Serve, inform, follow up with value. Desperation destroys positioning.

## Sales Quality Standards

### Pipeline Metrics to Track

| Metric                      | Target                              |
|-----------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Lead response time          | < 4 business hours                  |
| Discovery call booking rate | > 50% of qualified leads            |
| Proposal-to-close rate      | > 40%                               |
| Average deal size           | Trending up quarter over quarter    |
| Sales cycle length          | 14–30 days (varies by project size) |

### What Good Looks Like

- Every lead gets a response within 4 hours
- Every discovery call produces a clear next step
- Every proposal is delivered within 5 business days
- Every onboarding makes the client feel confident in their decision
- Every lost deal is documented with the reason why

## Lead Qualification Scorecard

Score every inbound lead before investing discovery time:

| Signal | Score | Criteria |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Clear business goal | +3 | They can articulate a specific outcome, not just "new website" |
| Budget aligned | +3 | Budget range is within our minimum ($10K+) |
| Decision-maker involved | +2 | The person inquiring can approve the project |
| Realistic timeline | +2 | 4+ weeks for standard projects, 8+ for complex |
| Referral source | +1 | Came through a referral or existing client |
| Competitor shopping | -2 | Sent the same brief to 5+ agencies |
| No defined goal | -3 | "We just need a website" with no business context |
| Budget far below minimum | -3 | Looking for $500–$2,000 work |

**Score interpretation:**
- **8–11:** Strong lead — book discovery call immediately
- **4–7:** Moderate — send qualifying questions before booking
- **0–3:** Weak — send resources, add to nurture, do not book call
- **Below 0:** Disqualify — polite decline with referral to a better fit
