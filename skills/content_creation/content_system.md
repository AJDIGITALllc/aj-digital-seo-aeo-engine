## Principles

### 1. Content Is a Conversion Tool

Every piece of content exists to move someone closer to a business goal. Content without a purpose is noise.

### 2. Clarity Beats Cleverness

Clear, direct messaging outperforms clever wordplay in conversion testing. "We Build Websites That Convert" beats "Crafting Digital Experiences for the Modern Era" every time.

### 3. Structure Before Words

The order in which information appears matters more than the quality of any individual sentence. Get the hierarchy right first, then polish the prose.

### 4. Write for Scanners First

80% of web visitors scan rather than read. Content must communicate through headings, bold text, and visual hierarchy before it earns a full read.

### 5. Every Page Answers a Question

Whether explicitly or implicitly, every page answers a question the visitor arrived with. Identify that question and answer it in the first two sentences.

## Content Strategy Framework

### Content Pillars

Every client needs 3–5 content pillars — core topics that align with:

1. **What they sell** (services/products)
2. **What they know** (expertise/thought leadership)
3. **What their audience searches for** (SEO/AEO alignment)

Example for a web agency:
- Web Design & Development
- Conversion Rate Optimization
- SEO & Digital Visibility
- Business Growth Strategy

### Content Types by Funnel Stage

| Stage          | Visitor Intent              | Content Type                        |
|----------------|----------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Awareness      | "I have a problem"          | Blog posts, guides, educational content |
| Consideration  | "What are my options?"      | Service pages, comparisons, case studies |
| Decision       | "Is this the right choice?" | Testimonials, pricing, FAQ, guarantees |
| Action         | "I'm ready to buy"         | Landing pages, CTAs, contact forms  |

### Content Mapping Per Page

Before writing any page, define:

| Element              | What to Define                            |
|----------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| Page purpose         | What action should the visitor take?      |
| Target persona       | Who is this page written for?             |
| Search intent        | What query brings someone to this page?   |
| Primary keyword      | What keyword does this page target?       |
| Messaging hierarchy  | What's said first, second, third?         |
| Proof points         | What evidence supports the claims?        |
| Objections           | What concerns does this page need to address? |

## Content-to-Conversion Framework

### Headline Formula

```
[Outcome the audience wants] + [For whom] + [Differentiator or timeframe]
```

Examples:
- "Websites That Convert Visitors Into Customers — Built for B2B Companies"
- "Grow Your Revenue With Data-Driven Web Design"
- "From First Click to Signed Contract in 30 Days"

### CTA Copy Rules

| CTA Type          | Pattern                      | Example                      |
|-------------------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Primary (lead gen)| Action verb + benefit         | "Get Your Free Audit"        |
| Primary (purchase)| Action verb + product         | "Start Your Free Trial"      |
| Secondary         | Lower commitment action       | "See How It Works"           |
| Tertiary          | Informational                 | "Learn More About Pricing"   |

**Never use:** "Submit," "Click Here," "Learn More" (without context)

### Social Proof Copy

Testimonials should include:
- A specific result ("43% increase in leads within 60 days")
- The person's name, title, and company
- A quote that addresses a common objection

Format:
```
"[Specific result or transformation]. [What working with the company was like]."
— [Name], [Title] at [Company]
```

## SEO Content Integration

### On-Page Content Rules

1. **Title tag:** Primary keyword + benefit, under 60 characters
2. **Meta description:** Action-oriented summary that earns the click, under 155 characters
3. **H1:** Contains primary keyword, front-loaded, benefit-oriented
4. **First paragraph:** Contains primary keyword naturally within the first 100 words
5. **H2 headings:** Target secondary keywords and related questions
6. **Body content:** Naturally includes semantic variations (LSI keywords)
7. **Internal links:** 3–5 per page with descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
8. **Word count:** Match or exceed the top 3 ranking pages for the target keyword

### Content Depth Model

| Content Type     | Minimum Word Count | Target Depth                      |
|------------------|--------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Service page     | 800–1500 words     | Comprehensive service explanation |
| Landing page     | 500–1000 words     | Focused on conversion, not volume |
| Blog post        | 1500–3000 words    | Thorough topic coverage           |
| Case study       | 800–1500 words     | Story + data + outcome            |
| FAQ section      | 500–1000 words     | 5–7 questions, thorough answers   |

## AEO Content Structure

### Writing for AI Extraction

AI search engines extract content differently than traditional search crawlers. They favor:

1. **Direct answers** as the first sentence of each section
2. **Structured formats** (lists, tables, step-by-step)
3. **Entity clarity** (who, what, where, when, how much)
4. **Comprehensive coverage** of a topic in one place
5. **Factual, specific statements** over vague marketing language

### AEO Content Patterns

**Definition pattern:**
```
## What is [topic]?

[Topic] is [clear definition in one sentence]. It [primary function] by [method].
[2-3 supporting sentences with specific details].
```

**Process pattern:**
```
## How does [process] work?

[Process] works in [number] steps:

1. **[Step name]:** [What happens and why]
2. **[Step name]:** [What happens and why]
3. **[Step name]:** [What happens and the outcome]
```

**Comparison pattern:**
```
## [Option A] vs [Option B]

[One-sentence summary of the key difference].

| Factor      | Option A           | Option B           |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| [Factor 1]  | [Specific detail] | [Specific detail] |
| [Factor 2]  | [Specific detail] | [Specific detail] |
```

## Content Quality Standards

### What Good Content Looks Like

- **Specific:** "43% increase in conversions" not "significant improvement"
- **Active voice:** "We build websites that convert" not "Websites are built by us"
- **Outcome-focused:** "Grow your revenue" not "We offer web design services"
- **Scannable:** Bold key phrases, use headings, keep paragraphs under 4 sentences
- **Attributed:** Claims backed by data, testimonials, or case studies

### Content Quality Checklist

- [ ] Does every section have a clear purpose?
- [ ] Is the most important information first?
- [ ] Are there specific numbers, results, or metrics?
- [ ] Is the tone consistent with the brand voice?
- [ ] Are headings descriptive and keyword-informed?
- [ ] Does the CTA match the page's conversion goal?
- [ ] Would a scanner get the key message from headings and bold text alone?
- [ ] Is there any placeholder or filler content? (Remove it)

## Content Production Workflow

1. **Define** — What page, what purpose, what audience, what keyword
2. **Outline** — Heading hierarchy and messaging flow before writing
3. **Draft** — Write the content following the frameworks above
4. **Optimize** — SEO/AEO pass for keywords, structure, and schema readiness
5. **Review** — Quality checklist and brand voice consistency check
6. **Integrate** — Place content into the design/build with proper formatting
